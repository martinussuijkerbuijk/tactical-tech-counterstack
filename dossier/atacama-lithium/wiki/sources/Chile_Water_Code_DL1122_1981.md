## Argument
The 1981 Water Code (Decreto Ley 1.122) privatized water rights in Chile, separating them from land tenure and creating a market in which private actors could accumulate hydrological resources without requirements for beneficial use.

## Why it matters to the Substrate Collective
This source is the foundational legal instrument of structural violence in the lithium triangle. It demonstrates how a dictatorship-era decree continues to govern water allocation decades after the democratic transition, embedding corporate extraction into the legal substrate.
