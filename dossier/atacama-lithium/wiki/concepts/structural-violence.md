# structural-violence

**Definition:** Violence that is built into social structures and manifests as unequal life chances, produced not by direct individual action but by institutional arrangements.

**Theoretical foundation:** Johan Galtung, "Violence, Peace, and Peace Research," *Journal of Peace Research* 6, no. 3 (1969): 167–191.

**Operationalization in lithium-triangle:** The 1981 Water Code (Decreto Ley 1.122) privatized water rights and separated them from land tenure, enabling mining corporations to accumulate hydrological resources while indigenous communities lost access. The violence is structural because it operates through the legal code itself, not through any single act of coercion.
