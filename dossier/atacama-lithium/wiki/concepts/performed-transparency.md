# performed-transparency

**Definition:** The strategic production of selective visibility — such as audited supplier lists, carbon-footprint summaries, and sustainability scorecards — that creates an appearance of openness while preserving core operational opacity. Performed transparency is not merely incomplete disclosure; it is a governance technology that substitutes the spectacle of openness for the possibility of accountability.

**Theoretical foundation:** Extending greenwashing critique and drawing on surveillance studies’ analysis of transparency as a modality of power. Where performed sustainability launders harm through virtue, performed transparency launders opacity through the promise of access.

**Operationalization in lithium-triangle:** We contrast Apple Inc.’s Supplier Responsibility Progress Reports and Tesla Inc.’s Impact Reports with the actual traceability of Atacama lithium, examining what is made visible (aggregate carbon figures, audit counts) and what remains hidden (specific water volumes, contract terms, community consent records).
