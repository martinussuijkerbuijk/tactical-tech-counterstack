# extractive-violence

**Definition:** The direct application of force against ecosystems and communities to remove raw materials for profit, including the displacement of populations, destruction of habitats, and depletion of water and soil.

**Theoretical foundation:** Drawing on extractivism critique in Latin American political ecology (Eduardo Gudynas, Maristella Svampa) and the concept of ecological imperialism.

**Operationalization in lithium-triangle:** Brine extraction in the Salar de Atacama by Sociedad Química y Minera de Chile (SQM), which pumps brine from underground aquifers into evaporation ponds, drawing down water tables and altering hydrological balances that sustain Atacameño communities and wetland ecosystems.

**Related concepts:** [[slow-violence]], [[structural-violence]], [[rentier-capitalism]], [[threshold-of-detectability]]

**Key sources:** Gudynas, E. (2010). The New Extractivism of the 21st Century. *Telos*, 158; Svampa, M. (2015). *Commodities Consensus: Neoextractivism and Enclosure of the Commons in Latin America*. Zed Books.
