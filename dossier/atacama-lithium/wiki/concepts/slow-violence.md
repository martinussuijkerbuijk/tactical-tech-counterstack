# slow-violence

**Definition:** Violence that occurs gradually and out of sight, a violence of delayed destruction that is dispersed across time and space.

**Theoretical foundation:** Rob Nixon, *Slow Violence and the Environmentalism of the Poor* (Harvard University Press, 2011).

**Operationalization in lithium-triangle:** Aquifer depletion, desertification, and salinization that accumulate over decades of brine extraction in the Salar de Atacama. The harm is not visible as a single event but as incremental loss of water for subsistence, livestock, and ceremonial practice.

**Related concepts:** [[structural-violence]], [[extractive-violence]], [[fast-violence]], [[temporal-asymmetry]]

**Key source:** Nixon, R. (2011). *Slow Violence and the Environmentalism of the Poor*. Harvard University Press.
