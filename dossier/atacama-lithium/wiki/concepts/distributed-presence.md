# distributed-presence

**Definition:** The condition under which a territory, its ecology, and its dispossession are simultaneously present in distant commodities, financial instruments, and consumer devices, yet materially absent from the regimes of visibility that govern accountability. The Salar de Atacama is present in every lithium-ion battery as a hydrological debt, yet this presence is scattered across supply chains such that no single node bears the trace of its origin.

**Theoretical foundation:** Drawing on Bruno Latour’s actor-network theory and the critique of commodity fetishism. Distributed presence is the obverse of fetishism: not the masking of social relations by the commodity, but the scattering of territorial relations across a technical network that renders them unlocalizable.

**Operationalization in lithium-triangle:** We trace how Apple Inc. and Tesla Inc. supply chain disclosures (and their absences) manage the distributed presence of Atacama lithium, and how the legal architecture of the 1981 Water Code distributes sovereign responsibility across state agencies, corporate boards, and international markets.
