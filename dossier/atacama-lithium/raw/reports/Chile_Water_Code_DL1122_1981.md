# Decreto Ley 1.122, Código de Aguas, República de Chile (1981)

**Source type:** Legal text  
**Publisher:** Ministerio de Justicia, República de Chile  
**Date:** 1981  
**Access:** Publicly available through Chilean legal databases (LeyChile.cl)

## Summary
The 1981 Water Code, enacted during the military dictatorship of General Augusto Pinochet, established a system of transferable water rights independent of land ownership. It created a water market in which rights could be bought, sold, and accumulated by private actors without requirements for beneficial use on adjacent land.

## Relevant to lithium-triangle
The code is the foundational legal instrument that enabled the privatization of water in the Salar de Atacama basin, establishing the structural violence that pre-positioned the territory for lithium extraction.

## Key provisions
- Water rights separated from land tenure
- Rights become tradable commodities
- No requirement for beneficial use
- State role reduced to registry and dispute resolution
